CNC'd Plastic Gasket

Introduction

The assignment was to create a plastic gasket for an aluminum part. We were given 1.5 hours to measure the part and create a drawing. This project was for ME360: Product Design. I used CREO Parametric 5.0 to design the gasket and GibbsCAM to cut it out using a CNC machine.

Results

The product worked well as a gasket, but was a little tight. Additionally, I designed the gasket to be milled using a 1/4" and 1/8" drill bits. At the shop, only the 1/8" drill bit was available, causing the radius of curvature to be half of what was necessary. This can be seen in the image at the top of the page.

Areas of Improvement / Learning Moments

From this project, I re-learned the importance of designing for manufacturing. I relied on specific drill bit being used for my design to work. This is poor practice and I'm glad I learned this on a class project as opposed to a real-world situation.

Additionally, I learned to trust my intuition when dimensioning products. This project forced us to dimension everything in one session and cutting out a gasket in one attempt. This made me obsess over getting an exact measurement from the calipers, such as 2.029 inches when the measurement was very clearly just 2 inches exactly.
